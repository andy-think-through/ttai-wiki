# Consulting

> Think Through AI's core service: paid discovery audits, solution design, and implementation for SME automation and AI.
> Last updated: 2026-04-08

## Content

Think Through AI's primary business is **bespoke AI and automation consultancy** for small and medium enterprises. The engagement follows a three-stage model designed to build trust progressively and prove value before commitment escalates.

### Three-Stage Model

**1. Audit** — Paid on-site discovery day at £300-£350 per day. Andy observes processes in situ, identifies manual and low-skill bottlenecks, and facilitates the client team to surface their own pain points rather than leading them to predetermined conclusions. This creates ownership and ensures work targets real friction.

**2. Solution Design** — Research and proof-of-concept testing, typically 2-4 days at day rate. Produces a clear roadmap including technical approach, costs, timeline, and ROI. Delivered as a proposal for the next stage.

**3. Implementation** — Building and deploying the solution. Charged at day rate by default; value-based pricing only where needed to win a deal.

### Commercial Principles

- **Pricing:** Day rate (£300-£350 depending on client relationship and complexity) is the default. Value-based pricing is used only to close deals Andy would otherwise lose.
- **Mileage:** 45p per mile (HMRC approved rate). Within first 10,000 miles/year threshold.
- **Payment terms:** 14 days
- **Client positioning:** Always position the next small stage, not the full engagement. This surfaces hesitation early and builds confidence through visible progress.
- **Process disclosure:** Don't reveal methodology prematurely in client communications. Let clients discover value first.
- **Focus:** Start with one department, prove value, then expand. Avoid multi-team "boil the ocean" pitches.
- **Trust-building:** Let clients surface pain points through observation, not sales questions. This positions Andy as listener and problem-solver, not as someone selling a predetermined solution.

Consultancy is client-facing, on-site work. White-labelling is out of scope (reframe as referral arrangements instead). See [[principles]] for positioning and communication style.
