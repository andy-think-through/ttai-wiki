## New Principles / Learnings

- **"AI as more staff" framing**: Pitching AI as virtual employees doing the boring stuff within existing systems (rather than requiring system overhauls) lands cleanly with non-technical clients
  - Discovered in: AccXel audit
  - Likely applies to: All consultancy engagements where client is anxious about disruption to existing systems
  - Evidence: Natalie bought in immediately; Charlotte and Dean adopted the framing throughout the day
- **Naming AI agents lands well with clients**: Gives a memorable, human handle for what would otherwise be abstract automation
  - Discovered in: AccXel (chose Bob, adopted Mark, adopted Sarah); previously KW Bell (Belle)
  - Likely applies to: All Mark and bespoke agent builds
  - Evidence: Charlotte and Natalie referred to "Bob" and "Mark" naturally throughout the afternoon session; created shared vocabulary
- **Let clients order their own priorities**: Surfacing pain points and letting clients sequence the work produces better buy-in than prescribing the order
  - Discovered in: AccXel audit
  - Likely applies to: All audit/discovery engagements
  - Evidence: Charlotte self-identified data hygiene as the prerequisite to the apprenticeship service agent; Andy did not lead her to that conclusion
