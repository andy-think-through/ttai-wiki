## Mark Workflow (v2)

### Stage 1: Lead Discovery (Browser-Based)
**Sources (in priority order):**

1. **PlanIt Warwickshire** — api.planit.org.uk for Warwickshire authority planning applications
2. **7 Council Portals** — Interactive searches on:
   - Rugby Borough Council
   - Warwick District Council
   - Stratford-on-Avon District Council
   - Coventry City Council
   - North Warwickshire Borough Council
   - Nuneaton & Bedworth Borough Council

3. **Developer Press Releases** — 9 major developers (IM Properties, Tritax Symmetry, SEGRO, St Modwen, Barberry Group, Indurent, Panattoni, Trebor, Stoford)
4. **Main Contractors** — 9 major firms (Winvic, Bowmer & Kirkland, Caddick, Willmott Dixon, Morgan Sindall, Kier, Galliford Try, BAM, Skanska)
5. **Industry News** — 9 news sites (Construction Enquirer, Construction Index, Construction News, Building, New Steel Construction, Insider Media West Midlands, The Business Desk, Place Midlands, Business Live)
6. **LinkedIn** — Restricted to validation of known projects or role identification (no feed scrolling)

**Target:** 7-8 qualified leads from last 10-14 days, within ~1.5hr radius of Rugby

**Output:** Markdown table with columns: Lead, Location, Category, Job Type, Signal, Fit Note, Date, Confidence, Source

### Stage 2: Contact Research & Email Preparation
**Sequential browser-based research (one lead at a time, NOT parallel).**

**Research Priority:**

1. **Personal Email** — Direct email for builder, contractor, project manager, site manager, development director, facilities manager, homeowner (domestic)
2. **LinkedIn URL** — For the named contact
3. **Phone Number** — Office or main line (no mobile unless published)
4. **Address** — Site address + postcode (site vs. registered office distinction)

**Email Template Structure (STRICT):**

| Element | Options |
|---|---|
| **Subject (choose 1)** | `Scaffolding at [address]` OR `Scaffold access for [address]` OR `Need scaffolding for [address]?` |
| **Opening (choose 1)** | `I'm Mark from Hawk Scaffolding Midlands.` OR `Mark here from Hawk Scaffolding Midlands.` OR `I'm Mark at Hawk Scaffolding Midlands.` |
| **Noticed (choose 1)** | 3 options starting with "I noticed", "I saw", "I came across" |
| **Capability (choose 1)** | 3 options describing scaffolding provision for [job type] work |
| **Close (choose 1)** | 3 options for low-pressure close |

**Signature:** Always `Mark, Hawk Scaffolding Midlands, https://www.hawkscaffolding.co.uk`

**Rules:**
- Do NOT mention Mike
- No sales language
- No call booking links
- No extra links or attachments
- Do NOT materially shorten/lengthen

**Output:** Markdown table with columns: Project Name, Address (Type), Job Type, Contact Name, Company, Role, Email, Phone (Label), LinkedIn URL, Source

### Stage 3: Outreach Execution
**Step 1 — Email Validation:**
- Use Hunter.io (free tier: 25-50/month) or ZeroBounce (100/month) or NeverBounce (1,000 new account)
- Mark as: VALID, RISKY (accept-all), INVALID, UNKNOWN
- Only send to VALID and RISKY; flag INVALID for manual review

**Step 2 — Send via Outlook:**
- Open Outlook Web (outlook.office.com)
- For each VALID/RISKY email, compose using drafted template
- If sign-in or 2FA required: STOP and request user takeover (do NOT ask for password)
- Track successful sends

**Step 3 — Summary Email to Mike:**
- **Recipient:** hawkscaffolding@outlook.com
- **Subject:** `(Mark [dd.mm.yy]) Today's leads emailed, along with phone/address/LinkedIn for you`

## Version History & Improvements

### v1
- Initial build
- Basic source rotation + contact research
- Inline tracker management (harder to maintain)

### v2 (Current)
**Applied seven improvements from Northants learnings:**

1. **Explicit-only scaffolding matching** — Removed inference; only leads explicitly mentioning scaffolding sent
2. **70/30 project size gate** — Mix skewed toward medium projects (large jobs felt unrealistic to Mike's team size)
3. **Mandatory tender portal searches** — Scaffolding never appears in planning data; must search actively
4. **Website-based email verification** — Preferred over SMTP/MX checks (more reliable for hitting right person)
5. **A-B-C subject line rotation** — Fixed sequential rotation (not random) to avoid "looks automated"
6. **50% contact rate hard gate** — If a lead has no named contact, it's not sent (lower conversion)
7. **Single persistent tracker** — Replaced three separate markdown files with one XLSX for easier deduplication + reporting
