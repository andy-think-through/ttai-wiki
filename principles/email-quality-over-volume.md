# Email Quality Over Volume
> Short, confident emails outperform long explanatory ones.
> Last updated: 2026-04-08

## The Principle
**Rules:**
- 4–5 sentences maximum
- No "just checking in"
- No "hope this finds you well"
- Don't reiterate costs unless asked
- Confident tone; avoid hedging

## Where Discovered
TTAI email drafting. Across multiple prospect emails and outreach campaigns, Andy consistently selects shorter, more direct variants over longer, more explanatory ones. Data from [[mark-lite]] cold email testing reinforces this: brevity and confidence convert better than lengthy justification.

## Where It Applies
- [[consulting]] prospect emails—establish credibility and next step in 4 sentences
- [[mark]] outreach templates—position value without over-explaining
- [[mark-lite]] cold emails to construction leads—short, clear, action-oriented

## Example (Bad → Good)
**Bad:** "Hi Sarah, I hope this email finds you well. I wanted to reach out because I've been thinking about the challenges that many companies in your industry face with marketing automation. Many organizations like yours struggle with creating fresh ad creatives quickly, and I think we might be able to help. We specialize in AI-driven creative workflows. Would you be open to a quick call to discuss? Thanks, Andy"

**Good:** "Hi Sarah, we help companies like [Company] create ad campaigns 3x faster using AI. Quick question: is creative velocity a bottleneck for you? If so, let's chat. - Andy"
