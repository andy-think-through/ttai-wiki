# Don't Reveal Methodology Before Paid
> Don't give away your approach before being paid for it.
> Last updated: 2026-04-08

## The Principle
Protect proprietary methodology. High-level frameworks are fine for trust-building; detailed methodology (specific tools, prompts, workflows) stays behind the paywall.

## Where Discovered
TTAI consulting—client communications and prospect interactions show a tendency to over-explain approach before engagement. This principle emerged from recognizing that early methodology disclosure reduces perceived value and dilutes differentiation.

## Where It Applies
- [[consulting]] sales emails—establish credibility without revealing the playbook
- [[mark]] demos—frame capability without exposing the prompt library or workflow details
- Any prospect-facing communication—trust is built through demonstrated results and structured discovery, not by walking prospects through methodology

## Exceptions
High-level framework descriptions are fine and actually build trust:
- "We use a three-stage approach: Discovery → Design → Implementation"
- "Our process combines audit, synthesis, and execution planning"
- Specific tools (e.g., "we use Claude for this"), detailed prompts, and workflow secrets stay confidential
