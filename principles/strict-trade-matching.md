# Strict Trade Matching
> Prospect-project matches must be trade-specific and size-appropriate.
> Last updated: 2026-04-08

## The Principle
Generic prospect outreach ("we work with construction companies") wastes prospect goodwill and tanks response rates. [[mark-lite]] and [[consulting]] conversions depend on trade-specific matching: a roofing contractor should see roofing-specific language, not generic "we help construction companies."

## Where Discovered
[[mark-lite]] Northamptonshire outreach — generic "we help construction businesses" messaging had low response rates. Trade-specific messaging ("specialist in roofing contract negotiation") showed 3x higher engagement. The prospect pool was the same; the matching precision changed the outcome.

## Where It Applies
- [[mark]] lead qualification (prospect trade must match service offering)
- [[mark-lite]] outreach email templating (email content must be trade-specific)
- [[consulting]] sales conversations (proof points must be from the prospect's industry)
- Any lead scoring or matching system (must filter on relevant dimension, not just company size)

## How to Implement
- Build a prospect profile: trade + size + pain-point signature
- Build a service profile: which trades you work well with, what company size you serve best, what specific pain points you solve
- Only match on >80% profile overlap
- Vary email templates, case studies, and proof points by trade

## Exceptions
- Portfolio expansion: if you're deliberately entering a new trade, loose matching can work (but accept lower conversion rates)
- Referral-sourced prospects: high-trust referrals can work even without tight trade match
- Relationship-dependent: if you have an internal champion at a prospect company, trade mismatch is less critical
