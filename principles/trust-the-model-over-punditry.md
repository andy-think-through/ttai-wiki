# Trust the Model Over Punditry
> Subjective overrides consistently lose money.
> Last updated: 2026-04-08

## The Principle
Narrative-driven betting decisions—skipping bets based on tanking narratives, motivation speculation, or "talent gap" commentary—consistently underperform vs. blind model following. The human story is compelling but wrong. The model is boring but profitable.

## Where Discovered
[[nba]] live betting—multiple instances of skipping statistically profitable bets based on:
- "Team A is tanking" (narrative doesn't align with win probability)
- "Team B has no motivation" (sentiment-based reasoning)
- "Player X is overrated relative to team Y" (talent commentary)

Each of these overrides cost money. The model's boring predictions had higher ROI.

## Where It Applies
- [[nba]] — all live and pregame betting
- [[tennis-atp]], [[snooker]], [[darts]] — any sport where narrative commentary is abundant
- [[consulting]] — client gut-feel ("we think this approach won't work") should be tested against data before overriding strategy
- [[mark]] — prospect narrative ("they won't be interested") should not override qualification model

The pattern: human narrative builds emotional conviction, but conviction and profitability are uncorrelated.

## Exceptions
- Hard constraints (e.g., injury confirmed by team announcement) override model
- Regulatory or compliance constraints override model
- When the model is known to be out-of-date or trained on stale data, narrative context can be valuable
