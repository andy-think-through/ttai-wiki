# AI as More Staff
> Pitch AI as virtual employees doing the boring stuff within existing systems, not as a system overhaul.
> Last updated: 2026-04-30

## The Principle
Non-technical clients are anxious about disruption to existing systems. Framing AI as "more staff" -- virtual employees that slot into existing workflows -- removes that anxiety. The pitch is additive (more capacity) rather than transformative (new systems), even when the underlying technology is transformative.

## Where Discovered
**AccXel audit (28 Apr 2026):** Natalie bought in immediately when Andy framed Mark as a BDM, Bob as an apprenticeship service assistant, and Sarah as a marketing assistant. Charlotte and Dean adopted the framing throughout the day. The "more staff" angle meant AccXel didn't worry about replacing HubSpot or Buds -- the agents work inside those systems.

## Where It Applies
- All [[consulting]] engagements where the client is anxious about disruption
- [[mark]] pitches to non-technical trades clients
- [[agent-browser]] positioning (agent works inside the client's existing tools)

## How It Works
1. Name the agent as if it were a person (see [[naming-agents-lands-well]])
2. Describe what the agent *does*, not what it *is* -- "Mark finds leads and sends emails" not "an AI-powered lead generation system"
3. Emphasise that existing systems stay in place -- the agent works inside them
4. Position cost as "cheaper than a hire" rather than "software subscription"
