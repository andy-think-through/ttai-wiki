# Position Next Stage, Not Bigger Commitment
> Always position the next small stage, not the full engagement.
> Last updated: 2026-04-08

## The Principle
Reduce friction and build trust incrementally. Instead of asking for a full 6-month engagement or £50k commitment, position the immediate next step: the audit, the kickoff call, the pilot phase.

## Where Discovered
TTAI consulting sales approach. This principle emerged from the realization that incremental positioning converts better and aligns with [[let-clients-surface-pain-points]]—small commitments allow clients to experience value before making larger decisions.

## Where It Applies
- [[consulting]] sales conversations—"Let's start with an audit" rather than "Let's do a full implementation"
- [[mark]] onboarding—position the setup call or first campaign, not the annual retainer
- Any prospect conversation where fear of commitment is a barrier

## Example Stages
**Consulting:**
1. Audit → Solution Design → Implementation

**Mark:**
1. Strategy call → Setup → First campaign

Each stage is positioned as the logical next step, not a down-payment on the full vision.
